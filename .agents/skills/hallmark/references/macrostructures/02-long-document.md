## 02 · Long Document

Reads like a memo, a letter, or a journal entry. No marketing structure. Continuous prose with inline section heads. The page is _literature_ about the product.

- **Heading:** inline with body — section heads emerge from the paragraph flow as small caps or bold short phrases.
- **Body:** single column, generous line-height (1.65+), measure 60–65ch.
- **Divider:** negative space; the gap is the divider; occasionally a centered ornament for emphasis.
- **Button:** typographic link inside a paragraph, not a separate block.
- **Image:** inline, sized to text measure; never full-bleed.
- **Reveal:** none. The page is just _there_.

Reach for it for case studies, founder posts, mission pages, products whose sale is _philosophical_. The brief is "tell a story", not "list features".

Avoid when there's a single decisive action to take — Long Document hides CTAs, which is wrong for transactional pages.

Reference: Frank Chimero's site, destroytoday.com, long-form Substack essays in product disguise.

**Sample opening lines** (imitate the _specificity_, not the wording):

> _"Saturday, 6:14 a.m. The dough went in at midnight."_ — opens with a time-stamp; the brand introduces itself as a moment in the day
> _"A monthly art publication featuring contributions by some of the most engaged thinkers working today."_ — e-flux.com/journal
> _"We design everything for everyone."_ — pentagram.com — refusal of the verb, treats design as universal practice
